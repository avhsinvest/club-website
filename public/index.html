We are AVIC

The Amador Valley Investment Club, which is a StreetFins club, is dedicated to providing an easy-entry environment that promotes and teaches students informed investing and financial literacy. The club will strengthen the understanding and provide resources for its members on the subjects of investing and finance.

We hold weekly meetings throughout the school year which all include activities such as a six-session introductory boot camp, a variety of workshops, mini-presentations, guest speakers, and virtual stock market simulations.

UPCOMING AND PREVIOUS EVENTS

ANNUAL STOCK MARKET GAME

Our annual stock market game (SMG) will begin in October 2021 and will last until April 2022. Participants will be given an amount of imaginary money to invest in stocks, bonds, and funds. Students may participate in groups of up to 3 members and will use investing skills they have learned throughout AVIC Bootcamp to make smart decisions with their money. It is up to you how much time and effort you would like to put into it. But the more you put in, the more you get out and have a higher chance of winning our prize.


This year, we'll be focusing on individual portfolios, specificially the reason behind different investments. At the end of the game, we'll evaluate everyone's portfolios through mini-presentations, and award the prize to the winner.

MENTORSHIP PROGRAM

Learning complex financial subjects may be overwhelming, that's why we are giving you the opportunity to learn through our mentors. This gives you individualized attention that you won't get in a crowded classroom.

  • Mini-Presentations
  • Written Articles
  • Portfolio Evaluation
  • Tutoring

You'll have the chance to learn more about any subject through tutoring through our mentors, evaluate your portfolio, and even share with the club through mini-presentations and written articles.

COURSERA

An overview of the ideas, methods, and institutions that permit human society to manage risks and foster enterprise. Emphasis on financially-savvy leadership skills. Description of practices today and analysis of prospects for the future. Introduction to risk management and behavioral finance principles to understand the real-world functioning of securities, insurance, and banking industries. The ultimate goal of this course is using such industries effectively and towards a better society.
